About Me

I'm Nysa, a sophmore in the Computer Science Academy at Morris County School of Technology. I am a student developer with a focus on Data Science and Artificial Intelligence. I like the challenge of turning data into something that actually works and looks good. Whether that's a career quiz in C++, an Arduino safety device, or a biomimicry website, I'm drawn to projects where code meets a real problem.

Outside of projects, I work at CodeNinjas teaching kids to code, which honestly taught me as much as it taught them. I've also held leadership roles in Girls Who Code, the MultiCultural Club, and Robotics Club. I'm always looking for the next thing to build or the next team to build it with.

4.
C++ Arduino

Arduino — AlertQuick

A pocket-sized safety device with an LED and buzzer, designed to be carried at night. Press the button and it signals for help, simple, fast, portable.

Problem Solved

Walking alone at night is genuinely risky. AlertQuick is a low-cost way to make noise and light fast. There is no need to unlocking a phone or open apps.

Technical Stack
  • Arduino C++ / Arduino Uno
  • LED, Piezo Buzzer, Push Button
  • Breadboard & wiring
My Role

I assembled the circuit, wrote the Arduino code, and spent a lot of time debugging why the buzzer kept firing on its own (spoiler: a floating pin).

Challenges

We had one loose wire and nothing works. Learning to systematically isolate components to find faults was a good lesson here.
